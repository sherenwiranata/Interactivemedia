The final week before submission was quite hectic, as I spent time finalising many aspects of the game fixing bugs, refining interactions, and adding last-minute touches to polish the overall experience. I also designed the Week 6 content page, which is inspired by vinyl records, incorporating visual and thematic elements that reflect that retro aesthetic.

Building on the sound-driven interaction techniques we explored in class, I created a voice-activated portal that only responds to specific voice commands. The idea was to make a button that appears clickable but actually isn't—adding an unexpected twist. Instead of clicking, the user has to solve a riddle and shout out a "spell" to proceed. I used an array of spell-like words (like "abracadabra", "open sesame", "thank you", etc.) as possible commands. The speech recognition runs continuously using the gotSpeech() callback, and once one of the correct words is detected, the user is granted access to the next page. The portal image playfully dodges the user's cursor using a floating div that repositions itself unless the right word has been spoken. This interaction adds a playful sense of frustration and reward, making the experience feel more like solving a puzzle rather than simply clicking through.
